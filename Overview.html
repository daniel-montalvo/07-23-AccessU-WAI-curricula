Scope and purpose

Role-based curricula addressing

  • trainers who want to include accessibility in their training
  • accessibility proffessionals who want to create their own training
  • HR and procurement proffessionals who need to compare and procure courses on Web accessibility

Audiences

  • Developers
  • Designers
  • Content Authors
  • Project Managers
  • Accessibility Testers

Currently available materials

  • Introduction to Web Accessibility -- First published December 3 2019
  • Developing Accessible Content -- Drafted
  • How to use this Curricula -- Expected to be published together with the curriculum for developers

Curricula Structure and Terminology

Each curriculum contains:

  • Modules: smallest unit. Can be combined to address specific audience needs
  • Topics: thematic gathering of module contents. Not advised to be taught independent of the module they belong to

Each of the modules contains:

  • Learning Outcomes: what students will learn and should be able to demonstrate
  • Competencies: previous knowledge advised for students and instructors
  • Teaching Ideas: optional ideas for teaching
  • Ideas to Assess Knowledge: optional ideas to support assessment
  • Teaching Resources: resources to deliver the learning outcomes
